## The speech therapy access gap

Over 7% of children in the United States experience a speech or language disorder. Yet access to consistent, high-quality speech therapy is unevenly distributed — limited by geography, insurance coverage, therapist availability, and cost. Many families face months-long waitlists, and even those in active therapy often receive only one or two sessions per week.

Research consistently shows that **frequent, structured practice** is what drives progress in articulation and phonology. A child who practices targeted sounds daily will improve faster than one who only works with a therapist once a week. But most parents aren't trained speech-language pathologists, and knowing *how* to guide a child through sound isolation, minimal pairs, or vowel blending exercises isn't intuitive.

This is the gap Wulo was built to fill.

## How AI voice agents work for speech therapy

Wulo uses intelligent voice agents — conversational AI systems designed specifically for pediatric speech therapy exercises. Here's how a typical session works:

1. **The agent greets the child** in a friendly, age-appropriate tone and introduces the exercise (e.g., "Let's practice the /r/ sound today!")
2. **The child speaks** — producing target sounds, words, or sentences as prompted
3. **Real-time pronunciation evaluation** — Wulo's speech recognition models, trained on pediatric speech patterns, analyze the child's production
4. **Guided feedback** — The agent provides immediate, encouraging feedback ("Great try! Let's make sure your tongue is curled back a little. Listen: *rrr*. Now you try!")
5. **Adaptive progression** — Exercises adjust in difficulty based on the child's performance

The exercises cover core speech therapy domains:

- **Sound isolation** — Practicing individual phonemes in isolation (e.g., /s/, /r/, /l/, /th/)
- **Vowel blending** — Combining consonants with different vowels (ra, re, ri, ro, ru)
- **Minimal pairs** — Distinguishing and producing words that differ by one sound (e.g., "wing" vs. "ring")
- **Listening discrimination** — Identifying target sounds in words and sentences
- **Sentence-level practice** — Using target sounds in structured phrases and sentences

## Safety and privacy for children

Building AI for children demands the highest standards of safety and privacy. Wulo is designed with these principles at its core:

- **COPPA compliant** — No personal data is collected from children without verified parental consent. Voice data is processed in real time and not stored beyond the session unless a parent explicitly opts in for progress tracking.
- **Parent-present design** — Wulo is built for parent-supervised sessions. A parent or caregiver must be present during practice, and the app provides parents with guidance on how to support their child.
- **No open-ended conversation** — The voice agent operates strictly within structured therapy exercises. It cannot be led into off-topic conversations, ensuring a safe and focused experience.
- **Age-appropriate interaction** — All language, tone, and feedback patterns are designed by speech-language pathologists for children ages 3–12.

## Supplementing, not replacing, therapists

Wulo is not a replacement for licensed speech-language pathologists. It's a practice tool — designed to extend the work therapists do in clinical sessions into the home environment.

A therapist sets goals (e.g., "work on /r/ in initial position at word level"). Wulo provides the structured, repeatable practice that helps a child build muscle memory and confidence between sessions. Therapists can review progress data to see which sounds a child is mastering and where they're still struggling.

The result: **more practice, faster progress, and better outcomes** — without adding to the therapist's caseload or the family's schedule.
